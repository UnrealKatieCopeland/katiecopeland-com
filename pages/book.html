The Invisible Barrier

How America's Courts Fail People with Disabilities — and a Framework for Fixing It

“A right that cannot be exercised is not a right. It is a promise the system made and then quietly broke.” From The Invisible Barrier, Chapter 1
About the Book

The Case for Court Access

Every day in courtrooms across America, people with disabilities are denied meaningful access to justice — not because the law permits it, but because the systems designed to protect them were never built to include them. The Invisible Barrier exposes the systemic failure of state courts to comply with ADA Title II and offers a comprehensive framework for accountability.

Drawing on legal scholarship, case analysis, and lived experience, Katie Copeland examines how courts confuse accommodation with advantage, how judicial discretion becomes a shield against compliance, and how the absence of infrastructure creates an invisible barrier that separates people with disabilities from their constitutional rights.

The book introduces The Copeland Questions™ — a practical framework of threshold questions that reveal exactly where a court's ADA obligations were violated. It provides judges, attorneys, court administrators, and advocates with the tools they need to identify noncompliance, implement meaningful accommodations, and build systems that work.

This is not a book about sympathy. It is a book about compliance. About the difference between having a right and being able to exercise it. About building the infrastructure that the law already requires.

Inside the Book

What You'll Find

The Problem

A systematic examination of how state courts fail to meet their obligations under ADA Title II — from physical barriers to procedural exclusion to cognitive access failures.

The Framework

The Copeland Questions™ — four threshold questions that any attorney, judge, or advocate can use to identify exactly where accommodation obligations were violated.

The Path Forward

Practical tools for building court access infrastructure, implementing meaningful accommodations, and creating systems that serve everyone — not just those who can navigate them unaided.

“The law already requires this. The harm is already happening. And the people who will change it are already in the room. Some of them are reading this sentence right now.” From The Invisible Barrier, Chapter 33c
Companion Resource

The Companion Workbook

Alongside The Invisible Barrier, Katie is developing a companion workbook designed for practical implementation. The workbook will include The Copeland Questions™ in worksheet format, self-assessment tools for court systems, accommodation request templates, and step-by-step guides for attorneys and advocates.

The workbook transforms the book's framework into actionable tools that courts, legal aid organizations, and disability rights advocates can use immediately. Details on availability will be shared with waitlist subscribers first.

An Invitation

Who This Book Is For

This book is for everyone who touches the justice system — and everyone the justice system touches.

It is for judges who want to meet their obligations but lack the framework. For attorneys who need to articulate what their clients require. For court administrators building systems that must serve everyone. For advocates who have been saying this for years and need the legal architecture to back it up.

It is for parents navigating family court with invisible disabilities. For social workers who see the gap between what the law promises and what the system delivers. For researchers studying access to justice.

And it is for anyone who believes that the law should work for the people it was written to protect.

Katie Copeland

Civil rights attorney, author, scholar, and advocate focused on access to justice for persons with disabilities. Creator of The Copeland Questions™ and the Family Court Access Act of 2026.
