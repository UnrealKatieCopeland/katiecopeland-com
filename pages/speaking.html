Speaking

Bringing Clarity to Complexity

Keynotes, CLE presentations, judicial education, and panel discussions on disability rights, court access, and systemic reform.

Katie Copeland brings a rare combination to the stage: the precision of a legal scholar, the authenticity of lived experience, and the clarity of someone who has spent years translating complex systems into actionable understanding. Her presentations are not lectures — they are invitations to see familiar systems with new eyes.

Whether addressing a room of judges, a bar association conference, a disability advocacy summit, or a university audience, Katie delivers content that is intellectually rigorous, practically useful, and deeply human. She speaks not from theory alone, but from the intersection of scholarship and experience that makes her work uniquely credible.

Signature Topics

What Katie Speaks About

  • 01

    The Invisible Barrier: How Courts Fail Before the First Request

    An examination of the systemic infrastructure failures — missing ADA coordinators, absent self-evaluations, non-existent notice requirements — that make court non-compliance inevitable. Based on her forthcoming book.

  • 02

    Access Is Not Advantage: Reframing Disability Accommodations in Courts

    Why the legal profession's instinct to view accommodations as "advantages" is both legally incorrect and practically harmful — and what a compliance-based framework looks like instead.

  • 03

    The Copeland Questions™: A Practical Framework for ADA Title II Analysis

    A hands-on workshop introducing the eight threshold questions that identify where courts violate their ADA obligations. Ideal for CLE credit and judicial education programs.

  • 04

    The Last Acceptable Discrimination: Disability and Family Courts

    How family courts use disability as a proxy for parental unfitness, the constitutional and statutory frameworks they violate, and what reform looks like.

  • 05

    Mistake Less, Believe More: Effect-Based Analysis in Disability Law

    Moving courts from intent-based credibility contests to effect-based compliance analysis — a paradigm shift that protects judicial authority while ensuring access.

  • 06

    Both/And: Holding Complexity in Advocacy and Life

    A more personal talk about the courage it takes to show up fully in spaces not designed for you — drawing on Brené Brown's work on wholehearted living and Katie's own journey as an attorney and advocate with a disability.

Audiences

Ideal For

Legal Professionals

Judicial Education

Judges, court administrators, clerks, and ADA coordinators looking for practical frameworks to improve court accessibility.

Advocacy & Academia

Disability rights organizations, university programs, policy conferences, and advocacy summits focused on systemic reform.

Working with Katie

What to Expect

Format flexibility. Katie is available for keynote addresses, panel discussions, CLE presentations, workshops, webinars, and podcast interviews. Presentations can be tailored to your audience and time requirements.

Substantive depth. Every presentation is grounded in legal scholarship and practical frameworks. Audiences leave with tools they can use, not just ideas they've heard.

Accessible delivery. Consistent with her commitment to disability access, Katie ensures her presentations are accessible — including providing materials in advance, using clear visual design, and accommodating diverse learning needs.

CLE eligibility. Many of Katie's presentations qualify for Continuing Legal Education credit. She works with organizers to meet CLE requirements for their jurisdiction.
